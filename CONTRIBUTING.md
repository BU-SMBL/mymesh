# MyMesh Contributing Guidelines

The MyMesh community is grateful for contributions in the form of issues (e.g. bug reports, feature requests) or pull requests. 
Before making a pull request, it's recommended you first raise an issue or contact the project manager directly (toj@bu.edu) to ensure any contributions are in-line with the current and future directions of MyMesh. 

Any communications or contributions fall under the perview of the [code of conduct](CONTRIBUTOR_CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md).

Any contributions will be published under the [MyMesh license](LICENSE.md), thus by making a pull request, you affirm that you are authorized to make such a contribution (e.g. you employer or institution doesn't have any claims to the copyright).

At this time, we are not interested in any contributions that are written primarily or entirely by artificial intelligence.

# Conventions
## Versioning
MyMesh utilizes the semantic versioning (“[SemVer](https://semver.org/)”) scheme, where version numbers are formatted as “MAJOR.MINOR.PATCH”.

## Commits
Commits follow the “[Conventional Commits](https://www.conventionalcommits.org/)” scheme to support changelog generation via [commitizen](https://commitizen-tools.github.io/commitizen/).
